Tortilla

Description:

The tortilla de papas, tortilla de patatas or tortilla española - also called tortilla de papas in Latin America, the Canary Islands and areas of Andalusia - is an omelette (that is, beaten egg, curdled with oil in the pan) with potatoes, although it is also you can add more ingredients.

It is one of the best known and most emblematic dishes of Spanish cuisine, being a very popular product that can be found in almost any bar or restaurant in Spain.

Ingredients:

    5 eggs. 4-6 large potatoes boiled in water and salt and diced. 2 tablespoons of virgin olive oil. 2 diced white onions. 3 large tomatoes, diced. 2 tablespoons of Ketchup Sauce. 8 leaves of fresh Basil. Salt and pepper to taste.

Steps:

1. In a pan add oil, heat over medium heat and sauté the tomatoes, onion, and basil until tender.

2. Season to taste. Add the potatoes and mix until they are wrapped in the sauce.

3. Beat the eggs with salt to taste and add the ketchup sauce. Beat well until the mixture is homogeneous.

4. Pour into the pan and lower the heat to medium-low, put a lid on the pan.

5. Don't forget to check when to turn the tortilla until it is golden on both sides.
